General Questions

Y-Ultimate is a program that uses Ultimate Frisbee as a tool to teach life skills to children from under-resourced communities. We focus on developing respect, teamwork, fair play, self-control, and communication through the sport of Ultimate Frisbee.

Y-Ultimate operates in multiple under-resourced communities across India, partnering with local schools and community centers to deliver our programs.

Ultimate Frisbee is a non-contact team sport played with a flying disc (frisbee). Teams score by catching the disc in the opposing team's end zone. The sport emphasizes self-officiating and "Spirit of the Game," making it ideal for teaching life skills and sportsmanship.

Participation & Joining

To enroll your child in Y-Ultimate:

  1. Check if there's a program running in your community or nearby school
  2. Contact our program coordinator or visit our website
  3. Fill out the registration form with your child's details
  4. Attend an orientation session with your child
  5. Your child will be placed in an appropriate program level (Foundation, Intermediate, or Advanced)

Registration is free for children from eligible communities.

Y-Ultimate accepts children aged 8-16 years. We have different program levels to accommodate various skill levels and age groups:
  • Foundation (8-10 years): Basic skills and introduction to the sport
  • Intermediate (11-13 years): Skill development and team play
  • Advanced (14-16 years): Advanced techniques and tournament participation

Program Details

Training sessions are held 3 times per week, typically:
  • Monday: 4:00 PM - 5:30 PM
  • Wednesday: 4:00 PM - 5:30 PM
  • Friday: 4:00 PM - 5:30 PM
Weekend tournaments and special events are scheduled throughout the year.

LSAS (Life Skills Assessment System) is our proprietary evaluation method that measures progress in five key areas:
  • Respect: Showing respect to peers, coaches, and opponents
  • Teamwork: Working collaboratively with team members
  • Fair Play: Demonstrating honesty and sportsmanship
  • Self-Control: Managing emotions and reactions
  • Communication: Expressing ideas and listening effectively
Children are assessed regularly, and progress is shared with parents and sponsors.
